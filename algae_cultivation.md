# Algae Cultivation Guide

## Recommended Species

### 1. Chlorella vulgaris ⭐ (Recommended for beginners)
- **Growth rate:** Fast (doubling time 2–4 hours under optimal light)
- **CO₂ fixation:** ~1.83g CO₂ per g dry biomass
- **Optimal temp:** 25–30°C
- **Optimal pH:** 6.5–7.5
- **Light requirement:** 2,000–10,000 lux
- **Biomass yield:** 10–20g dry weight/m²/day in flat-plate PBR
- **Uses:** Biodiesel, animal feed, CO₂ scrubbing, fertilizer

### 2. Spirulina platensis
- **Growth rate:** Moderate
- **Optimal temp:** 30–35°C
- **Optimal pH:** 9.0–10.5 (alkaliphilic — alkaline conditions)
- **Biomass yield:** 15–25g dry weight/m²/day
- **Uses:** High-protein food supplement, feed, pigments

### 3. Nannochloropsis sp.
- **Best for:** Biodiesel (up to 60% lipid content)
- **Optimal temp:** 20–25°C
- **Slower growth** but highest oil yield

## Growth Medium — BG-11 Recipe (1 Litre)

| Chemical | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| NaNO₃ (Sodium nitrate) | 1.50 g |
| K₂HPO₄ (Dipotassium phosphate) | 0.04 g |
| MgSO₄·7H₂O | 0.075 g |
| CaCl₂·2H₂O | 0.036 g |
| Citric acid | 0.006 g |
| Ferric ammonium citrate | 0.006 g |
| EDTA disodium salt | 0.001 g |
| Na₂CO₃ | 0.020 g |
| Trace metal solution* | 1 mL |
| Distilled water | to 1 L |

*Trace metal solution (per litre): H₃BO₃ 2.86g, MnCl₂·4H₂O 1.81g, ZnSO₄·7H₂O 0.222g, CuSO₄·5H₂O 0.079g, Co(NO₃)₂·6H₂O 0.050g, Na₂MoO₄·2H₂O 0.390g

> **Tip:** Pre-made BG-11 powder is available online (e.g., Sigma-Aldrich, algae supply shops). Much easier than preparing from scratch.

---

## Simpler Alternative: TAP Medium (Tap-water based)

For beginners, a simplified medium using:
- **1L dechlorinated tap water** (let tap water sit 24h or use aquarium dechlorinator)
- **1g Urea** (nitrogen source)
- **0.1g Triple superphosphate** (phosphorus source)
- **0.5g Potassium sulphate**
- Adjust pH to 7.0 with citric acid or baking soda

This works well for *Chlorella* and is cheap and accessible.

---

## Inoculation Protocol

1. Start with a clean, sterilized photobioreactor (autoclave or rinse with 10% bleach, then rinse 3× with distilled water).
2. Fill with BG-11 medium (or TAP medium).
3. Add starter culture at **10% v/v** (100mL algae per 1L medium).
4. Starter culture can be purchased online or from university biology departments.
5. Begin air pump circulation immediately.
6. Monitor OD₆₈₀ (optical density at 680nm) daily with a cheap spectrophotometer or by visual greenness.

## Daily Maintenance Checklist

| Task | Frequency | Target |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Check pH | Daily | 6.5–8.5 |
| Check temperature | Daily | 20–30°C |
| Check culture colour | Daily | Bright green (healthy) |
| Check flow rate | Daily | Gentle bubbling visible |
| Measure OD₆₈₀ | Every 2–3 days | Growing from 0.1 to 1.5+ |
| Nutrient top-up | Every 7–14 days | Add fresh BG-11 at 10% v/v |
| Partial harvest | When OD₆₈₀ > 1.5 | Remove 30–40% of culture |
| Full medium change | Monthly | Prevents nutrient depletion |

---

## pH Management

- **pH too low** (<6.5): Add sodium bicarbonate (baking soda) solution (1g/L)
- **pH too high** (>8.5): Increase CO₂ injection rate / reduce to fresh medium
- Normal healthy algae culture raises pH during the day (CO₂ consumption) and lowers slightly at night

---

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Culture turns yellow | Nitrogen starvation | Add NaNO₃ or urea |
| Culture turns brown | Temperature stress / contamination | Check temp, restart with clean culture |
| Culture turns white/clear | Culture crashed | Start fresh; check all parameters |
| Foaming | Contamination (bacteria) | Add anti-foam agent, restart culture |
| Slow growth | Insufficient light | Add grow LEDs; increase CO₂ |
| Clumping on walls | Normal biofilm | Scrub walls during maintenance |
| Foul smell | Anaerobic bacteria | Increase aeration, check for dead spots |

## Harvesting & Processing

### Step 1: Centrifugation (best)
- Spin culture at 5,000–10,000 rpm for 10 min
- Collect green paste from bottom
- Approx. 1–3g dry weight per 100mL of dense culture

### Step 2: Filtration (DIY alternative)
- Filter through 10–25 micron filter cloth or coffee filter
- Slower but works for small batches

### Step 3: Drying
- Spread paste on tray and dry at 60°C for 4–6 hours
- OR sun-dry for 1–2 days

### Step 4: Use
- **Feed:** Mix dried algae powder in animal/fish feed (up to 10% of diet)
- **Fertilizer:** Mix wet paste directly into soil (high nitrogen)
- **Biodiesel:** Extract lipids using hexane or supercritical CO₂ (requires lab equipment)
- **Biogas:** Add wet biomass to anaerobic digester
